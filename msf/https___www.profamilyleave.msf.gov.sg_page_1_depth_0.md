Government-Paid leave schemes for parents
Find out more about the benefits, eligibility criteria and application/claim procedures.
![note-book](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-illustration-women-notebook.webp)
Childcare Leave / Extended Childcare Leave
Supports eligible working parents (including self-employed, adoptive, foster parents, step-parents and legal guardians) in caring for and enjoying quality time with their children.
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Maternity Leave
Supports eligible working mothers (including self-employed mothers) in recovering from delivery and caring for their newborn children.
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Paternity Leave
Supports eligible working fathers (including self-employed fathers) in caring for their newborn children and encourages shared parental responsibility.  
  
From 1 Apr 2025, eligible working fathers of Singaporean children will be entitled to 4 weeks of paternity leave.
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Adoption Leave
Supports eligible adoptive working mothers (including self-employed mothers) in caring for their adopted infant (below 12 months old).
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Shared Parental Leave
Supports eligible working parents in sharing parental responsibility and caring for their infants.
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
2013 - Shared Parental Leave
Supports eligible working fathers (including self-employed fathers) in sharing parental responsibility through sharing up to 4 weeks of their wife’s Government-Paid Maternity Leave (GPML) or Adoption Leave for Mothers (AL) benefits.

![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Maternity Benefit
Supports eligible working mothers (including self-employed mothers) who do not qualify for the Government-Paid Maternity Leave (GPML) scheme due to their employment arrangements (e.g. on short-term employment contracts, or employment contracts had expired before the birth of their child).
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Paternity Benefit
Supports eligible working fathers (including self-employed fathers) who do not qualify for the Government-Paid Paternity Leave (GPPL) scheme due to their employment arrangements (e.g. on short-term employment contracts, or employment contracts had expired before the birth or adoption of their child).
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Adoption Benefit
Supports eligible adoptive working mothers (including self-employed mothers) who do not qualify for the Adoption Leave for Mothers (AL) scheme due to their employment arrangements (e.g. on short-term employment contracts, or employment contracts had expired before the adoption of their child).
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Shared Parental Leave Benefit
Supports eligible working parents who do not qualify for the Shared Parental Leave (SPL) scheme due to their employment arrangements (e.g. on short-term employment contracts, or employment contracts had expired before the birth or adoption of their child).
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
Unpaid Infant Care Leave
Supports eligible working parents (including adoptive, foster parents, step-parents and legal guardians) in caring for their newborn children.
![right-arrow-icon](https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/f/gstatic-ico-chevron-right.svg)
